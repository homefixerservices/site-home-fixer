Common Issues in Cork's Older Houses

Cork has many beautiful period properties and older homes with character. But age brings challenges. Here are the issues I see most often and what can be done about them.

1. Damp and Rising Moisture

This is probably the number one issue in older Cork homes. Many were built before modern damp-proof courses were standard. Signs include:

  • Tide marks on walls near the floor
  • Salt deposits on brickwork
  • Musty smells in ground-floor rooms
  • Damaged skirting boards

Solution: Proper diagnosis is key â€” not all damp is rising damp. Treatment ranges from improved ventilation to chemical damp-proofing.

2. Draughty Windows and Doors

Original sash windows and wooden doors are lovely but often let in cold air. This affects comfort and heating bills.

Solution: Draught-proofing can make a huge difference without replacing original features. New seals, brush strips and secondary glazing are all options.

3. Outdated Plumbing

Lead pipes, old stopcocks and inefficient systems are common in properties over 50 years old.

Solution: Gradual replacement is often more practical than a full overhaul. Priority should go to lead pipes and any visible corrosion.

4. Cracked Walls and Settling

Some movement is normal in old buildings, but new or widening cracks need attention.

Solution: Most cracks are cosmetic and easily filled. Structural cracks (usually diagonal and wider than 5mm) need professional assessment.

5. Worn Flooring

Original floorboards often have gaps, squeaks or damaged sections after decades of use.

Solution: Boards can often be lifted, gaps filled, and the floor restored. Complete replacement is rarely needed.

6. Poor Insulation

Solid walls and uninsulated attics mean heat escapes quickly.

Solution: Attic insulation is straightforward. Wall insulation is trickier in period properties but internal insulation is possible in some cases.

7. Dated Electrical Systems

Old wiring, insufficient sockets and outdated fuse boxes are safety concerns.

Solution: A qualified electrician should assess the system. Rewiring may be needed, but sometimes upgrades to the consumer unit are enough.

Living with an Older Home

These homes have character that new builds can't match. With the right maintenance and improvements, they can be comfortable, efficient and beautiful for another century.
