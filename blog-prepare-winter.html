How to Prepare Your Home for Winter

Irish winters bring rain, wind and cold. A bit of preparation now can prevent expensive problems later and keep your home warm and dry through the darker months.

The Autumn Checklist

Ideally, complete these tasks before November when the worst weather arrives.

Outside Your Home

Clean Gutters and Downpipes

Fallen leaves block gutters quickly. Blocked gutters overflow, causing water damage to walls and foundations. Clean them thoroughly and check for any damage.

Check Your Roof

Look for missing, cracked or loose tiles. Check flashings around chimneys and vents. A small problem now becomes a leak in January.

Trim Overhanging Branches

Winter storms can bring down branches onto your roof or windows. Cut back anything that's too close to the house.

Check External Taps and Pipes

Drain outside taps if possible. Insulate any exposed pipes to prevent freezing.

Inside Your Home

Service Your Heating

Get your boiler serviced before you need it daily. Bleed radiators to remove air pockets that reduce efficiency.

Check Draught Proofing

Feel around windows and doors for cold air. Replace worn seals and consider draught excluders for the bottom of doors.

Test Smoke and CO Alarms

With heating on more and windows closed, this is critical. Replace batteries and test all alarms.

Insulate the Attic

If you can see the joists, you don't have enough insulation. Topping up attic insulation is one of the best investments you can make.

Check the Hot Water Tank

Ensure it's properly insulated with a jacket. Check for any signs of leaks or corrosion.

Emergency Preparation

Know where your stopcock is and make sure it works. In a burst pipe emergency, you need to shut off water quickly. Keep a torch handy for power cuts.

Don't Wait for Problems

Prevention is always cheaper than repair. A few hours of maintenance now can save thousands in emergency repairs during the winter months.
