Flooring Options for Irish Homes

Choosing flooring for an Irish home means considering our climate — the damp, the temperature changes, the mud that gets tracked in. Here's an honest comparison of the main options.

Laminate Flooring

Laminate remains popular for good reason. It's affordable, looks good and is relatively easy to install.

Pros:
  • Budget-friendly
  • Wide range of styles and wood effects
  • Reasonably durable for everyday use
  • Easy to clean
Cons:
  • Not waterproof — swells if water gets underneath
  • Can sound hollow underfoot
  • Cannot be refinished

Best for: Bedrooms, living rooms, hallways (with good mats)

Luxury Vinyl Tile (LVT)

LVT has become increasingly popular in Ireland. It handles moisture far better than laminate.

Pros:
  • 100% waterproof
  • Softer and warmer underfoot than tiles
  • Very realistic wood and stone effects
  • Durable and scratch-resistant
  • Quiet to walk on
Cons:
  • More expensive than basic laminate
  • Quality varies significantly between brands

Best for: Kitchens, bathrooms, utility rooms, anywhere moisture is a concern

Engineered Wood

Real wood on top, with stable layers underneath. A good middle ground between solid wood and laminate.

Pros:
  • Real wood look and feel
  • More stable than solid wood in varying humidity
  • Can be refinished (depending on top layer thickness)
  • Works with underfloor heating
Cons:
  • Higher cost
  • Still sensitive to excess moisture
  • Scratches more easily than LVT

Best for: Living rooms, bedrooms, dining rooms

Solid Wood

Beautiful but demanding. Solid wood needs the right conditions to perform well.

Pros:
  • Timeless beauty
  • Can be sanded and refinished many times
  • Adds value to a property
Cons:
  • Expands and contracts with humidity changes
  • Not suitable for bathrooms or kitchens
  • Expensive
  • Requires careful acclimatisation before fitting

Best for: Period properties, premium renovations

My Recommendation for Most Irish Homes

For practical, everyday living in Ireland's climate, I often recommend:

  • LVT in kitchens, bathrooms and entrances — handles moisture and mud
  • Good quality laminate or engineered wood in living areas — comfort and style
  • Carpet in bedrooms — warmth and comfort (though not everyone agrees!)
