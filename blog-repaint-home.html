When to Repaint Your Home in Ireland's Climate

Timing is everything when it comes to painting in Ireland. Get it right and your paint job will last for years. Get it wrong and you might be doing it again sooner than you'd like.

Best Time for Exterior Painting

In Ireland, the window for exterior painting is narrower than in warmer countries. The ideal conditions are:

  • Temperature: Between 10°C and 25°C
  • Humidity: Below 85%
  • Weather: Dry conditions with no rain forecast for at least 24 hours

This typically means late spring (May-June) and early autumn (September) are your best bets. Avoid winter entirely for exterior work.

Interior Painting: More Flexibility

Good news — interior painting can be done year-round in Ireland. However, consider these factors:

  • Winter heating can help paint dry faster
  • Ensure good ventilation regardless of season
  • Avoid very humid days even indoors

Signs Your Home Needs Repainting

Exterior Signs
  • Paint is peeling, cracking or flaking
  • Colour has faded significantly
  • Wood is exposed or showing through
  • Mould or mildew visible on surfaces
Interior Signs
  • Walls look dull or stained
  • Scuff marks that won't clean off
  • Previous paint is bubbling
  • You simply want a fresh look

Preparation Is Key

Regardless of when you paint, proper preparation makes all the difference:

  1. Clean surfaces thoroughly
  2. Sand rough areas
  3. Fill holes and cracks
  4. Apply primer where needed
  5. Protect floors and furniture

Choosing the Right Paint

For Ireland's climate, invest in quality paint designed for damp conditions. Look for:

  • Exterior: Weather-resistant, breathable masonry paint
  • Bathrooms/Kitchens: Mould-resistant formulas
  • General interior: Durable, washable finishes
